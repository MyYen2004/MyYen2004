    Hand segmentation plays a crucial role in numerous computer vision applications, ranging from human-computer interaction to virtual reality. Recent research has focused on segmenting hands in natural images and videos, facing challenges such as significant variations in hand shape, size, texture, occlusions by other objects, and complex lighting conditions. Traditional methods often rely on local or global image features but struggle to handle complex scenarios and large variations. Modern deep learning methods, especially convolutional neural networks (CNNs), have made significant advancements in hand segmentation. However, these methods still have limitations in capturing complex spatial relationships between hand parts and handling difficult boundary cases. In this study, we propose a hand segmentation method based on deep convolutional neural networks, combined with a graph model to exploit spatial relationships between hand parts. We also employ data augmentation techniques to improve the model's robustness. Experimental results on standard datasets demonstrate that our method achieves competitive performance compared to existing methods.
